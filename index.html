Haugland’s profile is defined by efficiency rather than volume. He produces consistent goal output despite relatively low chance generation, driven by positioning, timing, and physical presence inside the penalty area. This creates a profile that is impactful, but also dependent on finishing efficiency rather than repeatable shot volume.

Movement Profile

The heatmap shows clear concentration inside the penalty area, particularly on the right side. Haugland operates almost exclusively in finishing zones, attacking crosses and second balls. He rarely drops into build-up, indicating a pure box-striker profile focused on end-product actions rather than ball progression.

Attacking Output

17 goals from ~10.4 xG (+6.6) indicates strong finishing efficiency. However, his xG per 90 (~0.30) is below most top strikers, confirming limited chance volume. He is not a creator or volume shooter — he is a finisher operating within defined attacking structures.

Defensive & Pressing Impact

Haugland’s defensive contribution is a major strength. Winning 11.2 duels per 90 and registering 3.9 recoveries in the opponent’s half places him well above average for strikers. He presses aggressively and contributes consistently in regaining possession, making him suitable for high-intensity systems.

Team Context

Haugland maintained output across two different environments. At Åsane, he played in a more direct, lower-possession system. At Hødd, he transitioned into a more structured attacking setup. The stability in output suggests a transferable profile that is not dependent on a single tactical structure.
