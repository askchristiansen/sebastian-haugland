Haugland is one of the most unusual striker profiles in the OBOS Ligaen. Despite relatively low chance volume, he produces consistent goal output through efficiency, positioning, and physical dominance. His statistical profile immediately stands out when compared to league averages: he operates with significantly lower xG volume than typical top scorers, but converts at a much higher rate.

Movement & Positioning

The heatmap shows a clear concentration inside the penalty area, with a strong bias toward the right side of the box. Haugland consistently occupies finishing zones rather than participating in build-up phases. His movement is based on timing runs into the box, attacking crosses, and positioning for second balls. This aligns closely with his statistical profile: low involvement outside the box, but high impact within it.

Attacking Output

With 17 goals from ~10.4 xG, Haugland significantly overperforms expected output. However, his underlying numbers show that he is not a volume-based striker. His xG per 90 (~0.30) is below most top scorers in the league. This creates a clear profile:

– Relies on efficiency rather than volume – Strong shot selection inside the box – Limited involvement in chance creation

Compared to OBOS strikers, he produces fewer chances but converts a higher percentage of them.

Defensive & Pressing Impact

Haugland’s defensive contribution is significantly above average for a centre forward. Winning ~11.2 duels per 90 places him among the most physically dominant attackers in the league. His ~3.9 recoveries in the opposition half further highlight his pressing impact. This suggests:

– High-intensity pressing ability – Strong physical presence in duels – Contribution to regaining possession

He fits well in pressing-oriented systems and adds value beyond goal scoring.

Profile vs OBOS Strikers

Importantly, Haugland has maintained similar output across two different teams (Åsane and Hødd), suggesting that his profile is transferable within the league context. However, the key question remains whether this efficiency can be sustained at a higher level, where defensive structures are stronger and chances are more limited.

Projection

At a higher level, Haugland projects as a role-dependent striker rather than a primary attacking focal point. Expected output:

– 8–12 goals per season in Eliteserien – Continued value through pressing and duels – Strong contribution in structured attacking systems

His effectiveness will depend heavily on:
– Quality of service into the box – Team structure – Ability to maintain finishing efficiency

Efficient, high-impact striker

Sebastian Haugland is a late-emerging striker defined by efficiency, physicality, and work rate. He does not drive attacking volume, but he converts opportunities at a high rate and contributes significantly out of possession. His profile is best suited to structured teams that generate consistent box entries and value pressing from the front.

Key risk: Output is highly dependent on finishing efficiency.
