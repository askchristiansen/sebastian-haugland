Detailed Performance Analysis

Sebastian Haugland represents a classic late-bloomer story in Norwegian football. At 29 years old, he has already made 43 appearances in the OBOS Ligaen (26 for Åsane and 17 for Hødd), logging 3,082 minutes. His goal-scoring rate has been remarkably consistent across both clubs: 0.51 goals per 90 minutes with Åsane and 0.49 with Hødd.

The standout statistic is his significant overperformance against expected goals: 17 goals from 10.4 xG (+6.6). This demonstrates elite-level finishing and clinical decision-making in the final third, rather than relying on high chance volume.

Equally notable is his defensive contribution. Haugland wins an impressive 11.2 duels per 90 minutes — exceptional for a centre forward. He excels in aerial duels (2.8 won/90), loose-ball situations, and records 3.9 recoveries in the opposition half per 90 minutes. These numbers highlight a forward who presses aggressively and contributes significantly to his team's defensive structure — a rare and valuable trait at this level.

With 13.4 touches inside the penalty area per 90, he consistently positions himself as a dangerous box presence. While his passing is functional (around 65% accuracy), he is not a creative playmaker (only 3 assists), confirming his primary role as a finisher.

Projection & Future Outlook

At 29 years old, Haugland still has several peak seasons ahead. His physical durability, high duel-winning rate, and consistent performance across two different teams suggest his profile is sustainable.

Realistic projection for Eliteserien (2026–2028):

  • Strong aerial ability and pressing will make him valuable in high-intensity systems
  • If he maintains 70–80% of his current finishing efficiency, he will be a reliable goal threat at the top level

Best fit: Mid-to-upper table Eliteserien side needing a clinical No.9 who also contributes defensively.
