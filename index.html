Detailed Performance Analysis

Sebastian Haugland is a classic late bloomer. At 29 years old he has already played 43 matches in the OBOS Ligaen (26 for Åsane + 17 for Hødd) and accumulated 3,082 minutes. His output is remarkably consistent across both clubs: 0.51 goals/90 with Åsane and 0.49 goals/90 with Hødd.

The most striking number is his +6.6 goal overperformance against expected goals (xG 10.4 vs 17 actual goals). This is elite-level finishing efficiency and suggests a player who is clinical in front of goal rather than a high-volume chance creator.

Equally impressive is his defensive contribution. Haugland wins 11.2 duels per 90 minutes – an exceptional figure for a centre-forward. He is particularly strong in aerial duels (2.8 won/90) and loose-ball duels, and records 3.9 recoveries in the opposition half per 90. These numbers show a forward who presses aggressively and contributes like an extra midfielder when out of possession – a rare and highly valuable trait.

He averages 13.4 touches inside the penalty area per 90, confirming he is a genuine box presence who knows how to find dangerous positions. His passing is functional (65 % accuracy) but not creative (only 3 assists), reinforcing that he is a finisher first and foremost.

Projection & Future Outlook

At 29 years old Haugland still has 3–5 strong seasons ahead of him. His physical robustness, high duel-winning rate and consistent goal output across two different teams suggest his profile is sustainable rather than a one-season wonder.

Realistic Eliteserien projection (2026–2028):

  • 10–15 goals per season as a starting striker or impact substitute
  • Strong pressing and duelling ability will make him a perfect fit for teams that play with high intensity
  • If he maintains even 70–80 % of his current finishing efficiency he will be a very reliable goal threat

Best fit: A mid-to-upper Eliteserien side that needs a clinical No. 9 who can also contribute defensively and win aerial battles.

Ready for the Eliteserien
